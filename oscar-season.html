01 —

the brief

The Oscar season campaign needed a face — a single illustrated character who could front the whole run, from cinema posters and outdoor advertising to a daily Facebook content series. He had to be flexible enough to live across formats, but recognisable in a single glance.

The brief was as much about building a community as moving tickets: could an illustrated character become someone people actually wanted to follow on social through awards season?

02 —

what I did

Character design

Designed the lead character — silhouette, palette, expression set — and a system for posing him across awards-season scenarios.

OOH artwork

Illustrations adapted for buses, billboards and cinema screens — same character, different formats and crops.

Daily social content

Facebook posts featuring the character reacting to nominations, big moments, and red-carpet looks — feeding the daily content engine.

Community building

Tone of voice, comment hooks and weekly drops that turned viewers into followers, then followers into a community.

selected pieces

03 —

the outcome

"Drove the Facebook community to over 363,000 followers."

The character became the page's most consistent engagement driver across awards season — a daily reason for people to come back. The campaign also set the tone for how the brand could keep using illustrated content as a long-term community anchor, not just a one-off launch asset.
