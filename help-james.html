case study 04 —

Help James
find Maria

an interactive video mystery, filmed in Lisbon, that ran on Facebook.

Concept Scripting Game mechanics On-location production
Year
2021
Client
Cork Airport
Format
Interactive video

01 —

the brief

Cork Airport wanted to push Lisbon as the must-book route — but competing on price alone wouldn't cut it. The ask: build something more than a static ad. Make people play.

So we built a story. A suitcase mix-up. A traveller named James trying to find a stranger named Maria across Lisbon, dropping clues in short videos on Facebook. The audience helped solve it — and the prize was a real trip.

02 —

what I did

Concept & story

Built the suitcase-mix-up premise, mapped out the story arc, and designed the clue-by-clue path to the reveal.

Scripts & mechanics

Wrote each video instalment, designed the participation mechanics on Facebook, and the prize-redemption flow.

On-location production

Filmed across Lisbon — Praça do Comércio, Sintra, Belém, the Alfama backstreets — directing each shoot day.

Edit & rollout

Cut the episodes, scheduled the drops, and tuned the comments-led detective work as the campaign played out live.

campaign moments

03 —

the outcome

"A campaign people actually wanted to watch — and play — to the end."

Episode by episode, the audience treated comments like a detective board: tagging friends, theorising, and tracking clues across the city. The format reframed Cork Airport's communication from "here's a route" to "here's a story we're telling together."
